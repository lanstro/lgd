  <Title>Part 1-Preliminary</Title>
      <Section>
        <Title>1  Short title</Title>
        <p>		This Act may be cited as the <Reference>Acts Interpretation Act 1901</Reference>.</p>
      </Section>
      <Section>
        <Title>2  Application of Act</Title>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(1)	This Act applies to all Acts (including this Act).</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Note><Defined_term>Note:	This Act also applies to legislative instruments and other instruments made under an Act</Defined_term>: see subsection 13(1) of the <Reference>Legislative Instruments Act 2003</Reference> and subsection 46(1) of this Act.</Note>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(2)	However, the application of this Act or a provision of this Act to an Act or a provision of an Act is subject to a contrary intention.</p>
        </Subs_1>
      </Section>
      <Section>
        <Title>2A  Binding the Crown</Title>
        <p>		This Act binds the Crown in each of its capacities.</p>
      </Section>

      <Section>
        <Title>2C  References to persons</Title>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(1)	In any Act, expressions used to denote persons generally (such as “person”, “party”, “someone”, “anyone”, “no one”, “one”, “another” and “whoever”), include a body politic or corporate as well as an individual.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(2)	Express references in an Act to companies, corporations or bodies corporate do not imply that expressions in that Act, of the kind mentioned in subsection (1), do not include companies, corporations or bodies corporate.</p>
        </Subs_1>
      </Section>
      <Section>
        <Title>2D  References to de facto partners</Title>
        <p><Defined_term>		For the purposes of a provision of an Act that is a provision in which de facto partner</Defined_term> has the meaning given by this Act, a person is the de facto partner of another person (whether of the same sex or a different sex) if:</p>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(a)	the person is in a registered relationship with the other person under section 2E; or</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(b)	the person is in a de facto relationship with the other person under section 2F.</p>
          </Subs_2>
      </Section>
      <Section>
        <Title>2E  Registered relationships</Title>
        <p>		For the purposes of paragraph 2D(a), a person is in a registered relationship with another person if the relationship between the persons is registered under a prescribed law of a State or Territory as a prescribed kind of relationship.</p>
      </Section>
      <Section>
        <Title>2F  De facto relationships</Title>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(1)	For the purposes of paragraph 2D(b), a person is in a de facto relationship with another person if the persons:</p>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(a)	are not legally married to each other; and</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(b)	are not related by family (see subsection (6)); and</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(c)	have a relationship as a couple living together on a genuine domestic basis.</p>
          </Subs_2>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(2)	In determining for the purposes of paragraph (1)(c) whether 2 persons have a relationship as a couple, all the circumstances of their relationship are to be taken into account, including any or all of the following circumstances:</p>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(a)	the duration of the relationship;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(b)	the nature and extent of their common residence;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(c)	whether a sexual relationship exists;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(d)	the degree of financial dependence or interdependence, and any arrangements for financial support, between them;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(e)	the ownership, use and acquisition of their property;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(f)	the degree of mutual commitment to a shared life;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(g)	the care and support of children;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(h)	the reputation and public aspects of the relationship.</p>
          </Subs_2>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(3)	No particular finding in relation to any circumstance mentioned in subsection (2) is necessary in determining whether 2 persons have a relationship as a couple for the purposes of paragraph (1)(c).</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(4)	For the purposes of paragraph (1)(c), the persons are taken to be living together on a genuine domestic basis if the persons are not living together on a genuine domestic basis only because of:</p>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(a)	a temporary absence from each other; or</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(b)	illness or infirmity of either or both of them.</p>
          </Subs_2>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(5)	For the purposes of subsection (1), a de facto relationship can exist even if one of the persons is legally married to someone else or is in a registered relationship (within the meaning of section 2E) with someone else or is in another de facto relationship.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(6)	For the purposes of paragraph (1)(b), 2 persons are related by family if:</p>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(a)	one is the child (including an adopted child) of the other; or</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(b)	one is another descendant of the other (even if the relationship between them is traced through an adoptive parent); or</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(c)	they have a parent in common (who may be an adoptive parent of either or both of them).</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <p>For this purpose, disregard whether an adoption is declared void or has ceased to have effect.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subs_1>
          <p><Defined_term>	(7)	For the purposes of subsection (6), adopted</Defined_term> means adopted under the law of any place (whether in or out of Australia) relating to the adoption of children.</p>
        </Subs_1>
      </Section>
      <Section>
        <Title>2G  Months</Title>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(1)	In any Act, <Defined_term>month</Defined_term> means a period:</p>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(a)	starting at the start of any day of one of the calendar months; and</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(b)	ending:</p>
            <Subs_3>
              <p>	(i)	immediately before the start of the corresponding day of the next calendar month; or</p>
            </Subs_3>
            <Subs_3>
              <p>	(ii)	if there is no such day—at the end of the next calendar month.</p>
            </Subs_3>
            <p>Example 1:	A month starting on 15 December in a year ends immediately before 15 January in the next year.</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <p>Example 2:	A month starting on 31 August in a year ends at the end of September in that year (because September is the calendar month coming after August and does not have 31 days).</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(2)	In any Act, a reference to a period of 2 or more months is a reference to a period:</p>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(a)	starting at the start of a day of one of the calendar months (the <Defined_term>starting month</Defined_term>); and</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(b)	ending:</p>
            <Subs_3>
              <p>	(i)	immediately before the start of the corresponding day of the calendar month that is that number of calendar months after the starting month; or</p>
            </Subs_3>
            <Subs_3>
              <p>	(ii)	if there is no such day—at the end of the calendar month that is that number of calendar months after the starting month.</p>
            </Subs_3>
            <p>Example 1:	A reference to 6 months starting on 15 December in a year is a reference to a period starting on that day and ending immediately before 15 June in the next year.</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <p>Example 2:	A reference to 6 months starting on 31 October in a year is a reference to a period starting on that day and ending at the end of April in the next year (because April is the calendar month coming sixth after October and does not have 31 days).</p>
        </Subs_1>
      </Section>
      <Section>
        <Title>2H  References to law of the Commonwealth</Title>
        <p>		In any Act, a reference to the law of the Commonwealth, or to a law of the Commonwealth, does not include, and is taken never to have included, a reference to a law in force in a Territory so far as the law is so in force because of an Act providing for the acceptance, administration or government of that Territory.</p>
      </Section>
      <Section>
        <Title>2J  Documents commencing proceedings</Title>
        <p>		A reference in a provision of an Act to any one or more of the following in connection with court proceedings:</p>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(a)	a summons;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(b)	an information;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(c)	a claim;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(d)	a complaint;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(e)	a declaration;</p>
          </Subs_2>
        <p>is taken to include a reference to any document through which proceedings may be instituted in a court.</p>
        <Note>Note:	An example of such a document is a court attendance notice under the <Reference>Criminal Procedure Act 1986</Reference> of New South Wales.</Note>
      </Section>
      <Section>
        <Title>2K  Rules of court</Title>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(1)	In any Act, <Defined_term>rules of court, in relation to any court,</Defined_term> means rules made by the authority having for the time being power to make rules or orders regulating the practice and procedure of such court.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subs_1>
          <p><Defined_term>	(2)	The power of the authority to make rules of court</Defined_term> includes a power to make rules of court for the purpose of any Act which directs or authorises anything to be done by rules of court.</p>
        </Subs_1>
      </Section>
      <Section>
        <Title>2L  References to Australian Standards</Title>
        <p>		In any Act, a reference consisting of the words “Australian Standard” followed by the letters “AS” and a number is a reference to the standard so numbered that is published by, or on behalf of, Standards Australia.</p>

  <Title>Part 3—Commencement of Acts</Title>
      <Section>
        <Title>3  When Acts come into operation</Title>
        <p>		If an Act or a provision of an Act is expressed to come into operation on a particular day (whether the expression “come into operation” or “commence” is used), it shall come into operation immediately on the expiration of the last preceding day.</p>
      </Section>
      <Section>
        <Title>3A  Commencement of Acts</Title>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(1)	This section does not apply to an Act so far as it provides for its commencement.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(2)	An Act (other than an Act to alter the Constitution) commences on the 28th day after the day on which that Act receives the Royal Assent.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(3)	An Act to alter the <Reference>Constitution commences on the day on which that Act</Reference> receives the Royal Assent.</p>
        </Subs_1>
      </Section>
      <Section>
        <Title>4  Exercise of powers between enactment and commencement of Act</Title>
        <Subheading>Application of section</Subheading>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(1)	This section applies if an Act is enacted and at a time (the <Defined_term>start time) after its enactment the Act will confer power to make an appointment, or to make an instrument of a legislative or administrative character (including rules, regulations or by laws</Defined_term>), because:</p>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(a)	the Act will commence at the start time; or</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(b)	the <Reference>Act will be amended at the start time by an Act</Reference> that has been enacted and that commences at that time.</p>
          </Subs_2>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subheading>Exercise of power before start time</Subheading>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(2)	The power may be exercised before the start time as if the relevant commencement had occurred.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(3)	Anything may be done before the start time for the purpose of enabling the exercise of the power, or of bringing the appointment or instrument into effect, as if the relevant commencement had occurred.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(4)	The exercise of a power under subsection (2) does not confer a power or right or impose an obligation on a person before the relevant commencement except so far as is necessary or convenient for the purpose of:</p>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(a)	bringing the appointment or instrument into effect; or</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(b)	bringing the Act conferring power into operation; or</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(c)	making the Act conferring power fully effective at or after the start time.</p>
          </Subs_2>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(5)	An appointment, or a provision of an instrument, made under subsection (2) takes effect at the start time or a later time specified in the appointment or instrument.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subheading>One instrument may rely on subsection (2) and existing power</Subheading>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(6)	To avoid doubt, a single instrument may be made before the start time partly under subsection (2) and partly under a power already conferred by an Act.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subheading>Extended application to provisions of an Act</Subheading>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(7)	Subsections (1) to (6) also apply in relation to a provision of an Act in the same way as they apply in relation to an Act.</p>
        </Subs_1>
      </Section>
      <Section>
        <Title>6  Evidence of date of assent</Title>
        <p>		The date appearing on the copy of an Act printed by the Government Printer, and purporting to be the date on which the Governor General assented thereto, or made known the Sovereign’s assent, shall be evidence that such date was the date on which the Governor General so assented or made known the Sovereign’s assent, and shall be judicially noticed.</p>
      </Section>

  <Title>Part 4—Amendment, repeal and expiry of Acts</Title>
      <Section>
        <Title>7  Effect of repeal or amendment of Act</Title>
        <Subheading>No revival of other Act or part</Subheading>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(1)	The repeal of an Act, or of a part of an Act, that repealed an Act (the <Defined_term>old Act) or part (the old part</Defined_term>) of an Act does not revive the old Act or old part, unless express provision is made for the revival.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subheading>No effect on previous operation of Act or part</Subheading>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(2)	If an Act, or an instrument under an Act, repeals or amends an Act (the <Defined_term>affected Act</Defined_term>) or a part of an Act, then the repeal or amendment does not:</p>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(a)	revive anything not in force or existing at the time at which the repeal or amendment takes effect; or</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(b)	affect the previous operation of the affected Act or part (including any amendment made by the affected Act or part), or anything duly done or suffered under the affected Act or part; or</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(c)	affect any right, privilege, obligation or liability acquired, accrued or incurred under the affected Act or part; or</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(d)	affect any penalty, forfeiture or punishment incurred in respect of any offence committed against the affected Act or part; or</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(e)	affect any investigation, legal proceeding or remedy in respect of any such right, privilege, obligation, liability, penalty, forfeiture or punishment.</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <p>Any such investigation, legal proceeding or remedy may be instituted, continued or enforced, and any such penalty, forfeiture or punishment may be imposed, as if the affected Act or part had not been repealed or amended.</p>
        </Subs_1>

          </Subs_2>

          <Subs_2>

          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(c)	if a provision of that law is repealed and re enacted or re made (including where that law is repealed and re enacted or re made), with or without modifications, a reference to the repealed provision extends to any corresponding re enacted or re made provision.</p>

      <Section>
        <Title>33  Exercise of powers and performance of functions or duties</Title>
        <Subheading>Powers, functions and duties may be exercised or must be performed as the occasion requires</Subheading>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(1)	Where an Act confers a power or function or imposes a duty, then the power may be exercised and the function or duty must be performed from time to time as occasion requires.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subheading>Meaning of may</Subheading>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(2A)	Where an Act assented to after the commencement of this subsection provides that a person, court or body may do a particular act or thing, and the word may is used, the act or thing may be done at the discretion of the person, court or body.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subheading>Powers, functions and duties of bodies not affected by membership vacancies</Subheading>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(2B)	Where an Act confers a power or function, or imposes a duty, on a body, whether incorporated or unincorporated, the exercise of the power or the performance of the function or duty is not affected merely because of a vacancy or vacancies in the membership of the body.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subheading><Defined_term>Power to make instrument</Defined_term> includes power to vary or revoke etc. instrument</Subheading>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(3)	Where an Act confers a power to make, grant or issue any instrument of a legislative or administrative character (including rules, regulations or by laws) the power shall be construed as including a power exercisable in the like manner and subject to the like conditions (if any) to repeal, rescind, revoke, amend, or vary any such instrument.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(3AA)	If a condition (the <Defined_term>ongoing condition</Defined_term>):</p>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(a)	must be satisfied before the making, granting or issuing of an instrument; and</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(b)	is capable of ceasing to be satisfied after the making, granting or issuing of the instrument;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <p>subsection (3) has effect, in relation to the repeal, rescission or revocation of the instrument, as if the reference to like conditions (to the extent that the reference relates to the ongoing condition) were a reference to the ongoing condition ceasing to be satisfied.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Note>Example:	A Minister may give a person a written permission if, among other things, the Minister is satisfied the person is of good character.</Note>
        <p>	This condition is capable of ceasing to be satisfied after the giving of the permission.</p>
        <p>	The Minister may repeal, rescind or revoke the permission in the like manner if the Minister ceases to be satisfied that the person is of good character.</p>
        <Subheading>Scope of powers in respect of matters</Subheading>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(3A)	Where an Act confers a power to make, grant or issue any instrument of a legislative or administrative character (including rules, regulations or by laws) with respect to particular matters (however the matters are described), the power shall be construed as including a power to make, grant or issue such an instrument with respect to some only of those matters or with respect to a particular class or particular classes of those matters and to make different provision with respect to different matters or different classes of matters.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(3AB)	If an Act confers on a person or authority the power to make an instrument (except a legislative instrument or a rule of court):</p>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(a)	specifying, declaring or prescribing a matter; or</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(b)	doing anything in relation to a matter;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <p>then, in exercising the power, the person or authority may identify the matter by reference to a class or classes of matters.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Note>Note:	This provision has a parallel, in relation to legislative instruments, in section 13 of the <Reference>Legislative Instruments Act 2003</Reference>.</Note>
        <Subs_1>
          <p><Defined_term>	(3AC)	For the purposes of subsections (3A) and (3AB), matter</Defined_term> includes thing, person and animal.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(3B)	Where an Act confers a power to make, grant or issue any instrument of a legislative or administrative character (including rules, regulations or by laws), the power shall not be taken, by implication, not to include the power to make provision for or in relation to a particular aspect of a matter by reason only that provision is made by the Act in relation to another aspect of that matter or in relation to another matter.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subheading>Power to make appointment to an office or place</Subheading>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(4)	Where an Act confers upon any person or authority a power to make appointments to any office or place, the power shall be construed as including a power to appoint a person to act in the office or place until:</p>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(a)	a person is appointed to the office or place; or</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(b)	the expiration of 12 months after the office or place was created or became vacant, as the case requires:</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <p>whichever first happens, and as also including a power to remove or suspend any person appointed, and to appoint another person temporarily in the place of any person so suspended or in place of any sick or absent holder of such office or place:</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <p>	Provided that where the power of such person or authority to make any such appointment is only exercisable upon the recommendation or subject to the approval or consent of some other person or authority, such power to make an appointment to act in an office or place or such power of removal shall only be exercisable upon the recommendation or subject to the approval or consent of such other person or authority.</p>
        <Subheading>Power to make instrument prescribing penalties</Subheading>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(5)	Where an Act confers a power to make, grant or issue an instrument (including rules, regulations or by laws) prescribing penalties not exceeding a specified amount or imprisonment for a specified period, that limitation on the penalties that may be prescribed does not prevent the instrument from requiring the making of a statutory declaration.</p>
        </Subs_1>
      </Section>
      <Section>
        <Title><Defined_term>33AA  Power to appoint</Defined_term> includes power to reappoint</Title>
        <p>		If an Act confers on a person or body a power to make an appointment, the power is taken to include a power of reappointment.</p>
      </Section>
      <Section>

          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(a)	for any appointment—the occasion for the appointment had not arisen; or</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(b)	for any appointment—there was a defect or irregularity in connection with the appointment; or</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(c)	for any appointment—the appointment had ceased to have effect; or</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(d)	for an acting appointment—the occasion to act had not arisen or had ceased.</p>
          </Subs_2>
      </Section>
      <Section>
        <Title>33A  Acting in offices or positions</Title>
        <Subheading>Acting appointments</Subheading>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(1)	Where a provision of an Act (other than subsection 33(4) of this Act) confers on a person or body (in this section called the appointer) a power to appoint a person (in this section called the appointee) to act in a particular office, then, except so far as the Act otherwise provides, the following paragraphs apply in relation to an appointment made under the provision:</p>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(a)	the appointment may be expressed to have effect only in the circumstances specified in the instrument of appointment;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(b)	the appointer may:</p>
            <Subs_3>
              <p>	(i)	determine the terms and conditions of the appointment, including remuneration and allowances; and</p>
            </Subs_3>
            <Subs_3>
              <p>	(ii)	terminate the appointment at any time;</p>
            </Subs_3>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(ba)	where the appointment is to act in a vacant office, the appointee must not continue to act in the office for more than 12 months;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(c)	where the appointee is acting in an office other than a vacant office and the office becomes vacant while the appointee is acting, then, subject to paragraph (a), the appointee may continue so to act until:</p>
            <Subs_3>
              <p>	(i)	the appointer otherwise directs;</p>
            </Subs_3>
            <Subs_3>
              <p>	(ii)	the vacancy is filled; or</p>
            </Subs_3>
            <Subs_3>
              <p>	(iii)	a period of 12 months from the day of the vacancy ends;</p>
            </Subs_3>
            <p>		whichever happens first;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(d)	the appointment ceases to have effect if the appointee resigns in writing delivered to the appointer;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(e)	while the appointee is acting in the office:</p>
            <Subs_3>
              <p>	(i)	the appointee has and may exercise all the powers, and shall perform all the functions and duties, of the holder of the office; and</p>
            </Subs_3>
            <Subs_3>
              <p>	(ii)	that or any other Act applies in relation to the appointee as if the appointee were the holder of the office.</p>
            </Subs_3>
          </Subs_2>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subheading>Acting by operation of law</Subheading>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(2)	If a provision of an Act provides for a person to act in a particular office (without the need for an appointment), then, except so far as the Act otherwise provides, while the person is acting in the office:</p>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(a)	the person has and may exercise all the powers, and must perform all the functions and duties, of the holder of the office; and</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(b)	the <Reference>Act or any other Act</Reference> applies in relation to the person as if the person were the holder of the office.</p>
          </Subs_2>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(3)	Anything done by or in relation to a person purporting to act in the office mentioned in subsection (2) is not invalid merely because the occasion to act had not arisen or had ceased.</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <Subheading>Definition</Subheading>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(4)	In this section:</p>
        </Subs_1>
        <p><Defined_term>office</Defined_term> includes a position occupied by an APS employee.</p>
      </Section>

      <Section>
        <Title><Defined_term>34  Power to hear and determine a matter</Defined_term> includes power to receive evidence and examine witnesses etc.</Title>

      </Section>

        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(1)	A Minister (the <Defined_term>authorising Minister) who administers (whether alone or jointly with one or more other Ministers</Defined_term>) an Act or a provision of an Act may authorise:</p>

      <Section>
        <Title>34AA  Delegation to persons holding, occupying or performing the duties of an office or position</Title>
        <p>		Where an Act confers power to delegate a function, duty or power, then the power of delegation shall not be construed as being limited to delegating the function, duty or power to a specified person but shall be construed as including a power to delegate the function, duty or power to any person from time to time holding, occupying, or performing the duties of, a specified office or position, even if the office or position does not come into existence until after the delegation is given.</p>
      </Section>
      <Section>
        <Title>34AB  Effect of delegation</Title>
        <Subheading>General</Subheading>
        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(1)	Where an Act confers power on a person or body (in this section called the authority) to delegate a function, duty or power:</p>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(a)	the delegation may be made either generally or as otherwise provided by the instrument of delegation;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(b)	the powers that may be delegated do not include that power to delegate;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(c)	a function, duty or power so delegated, when performed or exercised by the delegate, shall, for the purposes of the Act, be deemed to have been performed or exercised by the authority;</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(d)	a delegation by the authority does not prevent the performance or exercise of a function, duty or power by the authority; and</p>
          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(e)	if the authority is not a person, section 34A applies as if it were.</p>
          </Subs_2>
        </Subs_1>

          <p>	(2)	If:</p>

        <Subs_1>
          <p>	(1)	Where, by an Act or a law of a Territory, provision is made requiring or permitting the presentation (however expressed) of a paper to the Parliament or to both Houses, or to each or either House, of the Parliament, it is sufficient compliance with the provision, in relation to a House, if:</p>

          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>

          </Subs_2>
          <Subs_2>
            <p>	(c)	where the provision specifies a period within which the paper is to be presented—the presentation, or the delivery and recording, of the paper referred to in paragraph (a) takes place within that period.</p>
